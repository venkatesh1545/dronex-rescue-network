Safety First

Disaster Safety Guidelines

Detailed instructions to stay safe before, during, and after different types of disasters. Being prepared can save lives.

Flood Safety

Critical safety steps to follow during a flood disaster

Immediate Steps to Take

  • 1
    Move to higher ground immediately and avoid flood waters
  • 2
    Do not walk, swim, or drive through flood waters
  • 3
    Stay off bridges over fast-moving water
  • 4
    Evacuate if told to do so by authorities
  • 5
    Move to the highest level of a building if trapped

Essential Emergency Items

Water (1 gallon per person per day)
Non-perishable food
Battery-powered radio
Flashlight
First aid kit
Whistle to signal for help

After the Flood

Listen to local officials for information and instructions. Return home only when authorities say it is safe.
Roads may have weakened and could collapse under the weight of a car. Be cautious when entering buildings; there may be hidden damage.
Floodwater can contain dangerous debris, be contaminated with sewage, or be electrically charged from underground power lines.

Important Reminder

Every disaster situation is unique. Always follow official instructions from local emergency management agencies. These guidelines are general and may need to be adapted to your specific situation.

Earthquake Safety

Critical safety steps to follow during an earthquake

Immediate Steps to Take

  • 1
    DROP to the ground before the earthquake drops you
  • 2
    COVER your head and neck with your arms; crawl under sturdy furniture if nearby
  • 3
    HOLD ON to your shelter until the shaking stops
  • 4
    If indoors, stay away from glass, windows, outside doors and walls
  • 5
    If outdoors, stay in the open until the shaking stops

Essential Emergency Items

Emergency water supply
Non-perishable food
Flashlight and batteries
First aid kit
Whistle for signaling
Dust mask

After the Earthquake

Provide first aid for anyone who needs it. Call for help if necessary.
If you smell gas, turn off the main gas valve, open windows, and leave the building. Report gas leaks to the authorities.
